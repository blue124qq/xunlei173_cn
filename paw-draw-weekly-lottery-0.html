Further information

Terms and conditions

Battersea holds a weekly lottery, the Paw Draw Lottery. In addition to the weekly Paw Draw Lottery, Battersea holds a raffle - called a ‘Superdraw’ each Spring, Summer, Autumn and Winter. All Paw Draw players are automatically entered into the Superdraw Raffle at no extra cost, as well as Superdraw Raffle players who have purchased tickets.

Out of each pound you donate to the Paw Draw, 85p goes directly to the dogs and cats that need our help. We could not do this without you, so thank you.

In 2019, the total raised by the Paw Draw was £2,583,836.
This was distributed as follows:
84.96% - went towards fulfilling Battersea’s purpose
13.63% - went towards the promotion and running of the Paw Draw
1.41% - went towards prizes

In the Paw Draw, each lottery entry is assigned a unique number. These numbers are entered into a Random Number Generator (RNG) that has been tested and approved by an independent, Gambling Commission approved, third-party test house. The first lottery number selected wins the first prize, the second wins the second prize and so on until all 14 prizes are assigned a winner. There are no rollovers. The draw is held each Friday – unless the draw date falls on a bank holiday, in which case it will be held on the next working day.

Winning lottery numbers are published on the Battersea website weekly. Should they wish to, players can call the lottery helpline on 0207 627 9312 (Monday to Friday 10am to 4pm) to enquire if they have won. Prizes are paid by cheque and posted to the winners within 14 days of the draw. Winners may also receive a call from Battersea.

We reserve the right to use winners’ names for promotional purposes, such as ‘Mrs Smith from London’.

The Paw Draw is drawn weekly and the chance of winning a prize per entry is 1 in 3,845.

The prizes available are:
1st prize - £600
2nd prize - £100
12 runners up prizes - £5

The cost of Paw Draw Lottery entry is £1 a week (£4.34 a month by Direct Debit), players may purchase more than one entry. All entries are paid in advance.

Battersea Dogs & Cats Home may (without giving any reason or notice at its absolute discretion) decline to accept an application, cancel an existing subscription, or terminate or suspend the weekly lottery scheme.

Any cancellation of an existing subscription will not affect your prior purchase of weekly lottery entries nor your rights as a holder of such entries, providing that you have complied with the foregoing obligations.

The Paw Draw promoter operates a Complaints and Disputes Procedure which is available from this web page and which will also be made available to customers upon request or a complaint being made.

The Superdraw Raffle

Paw Draw Lottery players are entered into the Superdraw Raffle for free.

Superdraw Raffle entrants who are not Paw Draw players, pay £1 per entry and may purchase up to 50 tickets online and up to 40 tickets by post. No tickets should be sold by any individual in the street or on the internet.

In 2019, the total raised by the Superdraw Raffle was £1,001,316. This was distributed as follows:
77.95% - went towards fulfilling Battersea’s purpose
20.05% - went towards the promotion and running of the lottery
2.00% - went towards prizes

All entries to the Superdraw Raffle and the Paw Draw Lottery are entered into a random draw that is conducted 7 days after the closing date stated on each ticket. Any entries received after the closing date are treated as donations, there are no refunds. The draw is conducted by an independent party in front of two witnesses. The first number drawn wins the first prize, the second wins the second prize, and so on until all 23 prizes are assigned a winner. There are no rollovers.

We contact the winners by phone and letter within 14 days of the draw date and provide the prizes to them within 7 days of receiving acknowledgement from the winners. We reserve the right to use winner’s names for promotional purposes, such as ‘Mrs Smith from London’.

Four Superdraw Raffles are held each year and the chance of winning a prize per entry is 1 in 10,884.

The Superdraw Raffle prizes available are:
1st prize - £3,000
2nd prize - £1,000
3rd prize - £500
20 runners up prizes - £25 M&S gift card

Golden Tickets

For the Christmas Superdraw Raffle, we will issue free Golden Tickets to a limited number of randomly chosen players from across Paw Draw and Superdraw Raffle entrants. Those players, upon entry to the Superdraw Raffle and the return of their golden ticket, will be entered into a separate, Golden Ticket raffle to win £200.

In the Golden Ticket draw process, all entries are entered into the separate random draw, the first number drawn wins the £200. The Golden Ticket chance of winning per entry is estimated as 1 in 2000.

The Golden Ticket prize is: £200

Rules for both the Paw Draw and Superdraw Raffle

Players must be over the age of 18 years. Any entrants found to be under 18, will have their stake returned and automatically forfeit the right to any prize.

Players must reside in Great Britain, we are not licensed to accept entries from Northern Ireland, the Channel Islands or the Isle of Man.

As of 1st April 2020, credit card transactions will not be accepted for online or phone purchases of lottery or raffle tickets. This is in response to legislative changes by the Gambling Commission. We will continue to accept debit cards.

Direct debit guarantee

The Guarantee is offered by all banks and building societies that accept instructions to pay Direct Debits.

If there are any changes to the amount, date or frequency of your Direct Debit, DDPay Ltd re Battersea Dogs and Cats Home will notify you 10 working days in advance of your account being debited or as otherwise agreed. If you request DDPay Ltd re Battersea Dogs and Cats Home to collect a payment, confirmation of the amount and date will be given to you at the time of the request.

If an error is made in the payment of your Direct Debit, by DDPay Ltd re Battersea Dogs and Cats Home or your bank or building society, you are entitled to a full and immediate refund of the amount paid from your bank or building society.

If you receive a refund you are not entitled to, you must pay it back when DDPay Ltd re Battersea Dogs and Cats Home asks you to.

You can cancel a Direct Debit at any time by simply contacting your bank or building society. Written confirmation may be required. Please also notify us.

FAQ

Why play the Paw Draw weekly lottery?

Play the Paw Draw weekly lottery and you can win one of 14 prizes each week, up to the value of £600. Four times a year, lottery players will also be entered into a super draw where you can win up to £3,000. All profits raised from the lottery go towards caring for abandoned dogs and cats.

Can I choose my lottery number?

Unfortunately you cannot choose your lottery number. A unique number will be automatically generated for you when you enter the Paw Draw weekly lottery. Your unique number will be sent to you by post within 14 days of receiving your application.

How will I know if I’ve won?

We will notify you in writing within 14 days of the draw date and your winner’s cheque will be enclosed. You may also receive a telephone call to inform you of the good news, or you can call the lottery helpline on 0207 627 9312 (lines open Monday to Friday 10am-4pm) to find out.

How do I claim my prize?

Prizes are issued by cheque so there is no need to do a thing. Please remember to notify us of any address changes so you receive your prize without any delays.

How many lottery entries can I buy?

As part of our commitment to responsible gambling, we only allow any one person to purchase a maximum of 5 tickets for each draw.

What should I do if I want to make a complaint?

We realise that sometimes we might get it wrong and we truly value all feedback about our work. We believe that complaints are an opportunity to learn and improve and it is important that you tell us so we can deal with any situation as quickly as possible.
